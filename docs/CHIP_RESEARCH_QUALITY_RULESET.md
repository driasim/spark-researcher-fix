# Chip Research Quality Ruleset

Use this when a chip starts expanding its source base beyond a small hand-curated pack.

The rule is simple:

- do not optimize for more source material
- optimize for more doctrine-rich source material

## Good Research Content

Good chip research content usually gives the chip:

- a reusable claim
- a mechanism
- a boundary or failure condition
- enough specificity to influence packets, retrieval, benchmarks, or next probes

## Weak Research Content

Weak research content usually looks like:

- event updates
- product or company announcements
- status posts
- generic inspiration
- material that would require inventing most of the packet by hand

## Coverage And Depth

Every richer chip should track:

- coverage count by area
- doctrine depth by area
- overcrowded areas

Healthy policy:

1. fill true gaps first
2. deepen shallow doctrine second
3. avoid repetitive source expansion in already-crowded areas unless depth is still weak

## Discovery Policy

If a chip has bounded discovery or crawling, it should:

- use trusted seeds only
- reject weak or announcement-style items
- prefer sources that match current coverage gaps
- create drafts first, not doctrine

## Transfer Rule

Keep the policy generic in core.

Keep the actual source lists, tags, and domain vocabulary inside each chip.
