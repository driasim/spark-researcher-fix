# Agentic Marketing Bench Promotion Bridge

The benchmark in this chip is intentionally synthetic.
It measures structural fitness for a startup distribution operating system.

## What Benchmark-Grounded Means Here

Benchmark-grounded means the candidate has a coherent answer for:

- where demand is discovered
- how the offer is packaged
- how assets are generated and distributed
- how leads are captured
- how CRM and analytics are updated
- how the system learns from feedback

It does not mean the candidate has already proven CAC, payback, or retention lift.

## Promotion Stages

### 1. Exploratory Frontier

Use when the motion is vague, the offer is weak, or the workflow graph is incomplete.

### 2. Benchmark Candidate

Use when a coherent system exists and the next bottleneck is clear.

### 3. Benchmark Validated

Use only when the candidate clears the benchmark thresholds and has:

- explicit motion-channel-offer fit
- automation coverage beyond simple task chaining
- a visible feedback closure path

### 4. Real-World Pilot

Before live rollout, require:

- link attribution
- named conversion surface
- CRM stage mapping
- experiment owner
- weekly review cadence
- rollback condition

## Rollback Conditions

Rollback from promoted doctrine if:

- the live pilot fails to create attributable qualified pipeline
- support and survey feedback contradict the promised offer
- manual effort grows faster than system output
- the channel economics depend on unsustainable founder heroics
